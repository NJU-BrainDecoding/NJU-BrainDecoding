Abstract摘要

The Nanjing University Brain-Computer Interface Institute work “Duala: Dual-Level Alignment of Subjects and Stimuli for Cross-Subject fMRI Decoding” has been accepted by IEEE/CVF CVPR 2026. The work focuses on a key bottleneck in brain-computer interface applications: new-subject fMRI data are expensive to collect and difficult to scale. Duala aims to obtain stable and accurate cross-subject visual decoding using only a very small amount of fMRI data from a new subject.近日，南京大学脑机接口研究院关于跨受试者 fMRI 视觉解码工作 “Duala: Dual-Level Alignment of Subjects and Stimuli for Cross-Subject fMRI Decoding” 被人工智能领域国际顶级会议 IEEE/CVF CVPR 2026 录用。该工作聚焦脑机接口技术应用过程中“新受试者采集数据昂贵且难以规模化采集”的核心痛点，旨在仅依赖极少量新受试者 fMRI 数据的前提下，实现相对稳定且高精度的跨受试者视觉解码。

Research Background研究背景

fMRI visual decoding aims to reconstruct or identify visual stimuli from brain activity. It is an important direction for understanding human visual representations and advancing brain-computer interface applications. With the introduction of foundation models such as CLIP and diffusion models, single-subject decoding has achieved notable progress, but cross-subject generalization remains challenging.fMRI 视觉解码旨在从脑活动中重建或识别视觉刺激，是理解人类视觉表征机制、推动脑机接口应用的重要方向之一。近年来，随着 CLIP、扩散模型等基础模型的引入，单受试者解码取得显著进展，但跨受试者泛化仍面临严峻挑战。

The challenge mainly comes from two aspects. First, different subjects have substantial individual differences in brain structure and functional representations, making it difficult to directly transfer a single decoding model. Second, collecting large-scale, high-quality fMRI data for every new subject is costly, which limits the scalability of practical applications.这一挑战主要来自两个方面：一方面，不同受试者在脑结构与功能表征上存在显著个体差异，导致同一解码模型难以直接迁移；另一方面，为每位新受试者采集大规模高质量 fMRI 数据成本高昂，限制了真实应用中的可扩展性。

Research Method研究方法

The team further observed that existing cross-subject methods can perform well during pretraining, but adaptation to a new subject may disrupt the original semantic structure, blur category boundaries, and degrade retrieval and reconstruction performance. To address this issue, Duala proposes a dual-level alignment framework for cross-subject fMRI decoding.课题组进一步观察到，现有跨受试者方案在预训练阶段表现良好，但在对新受试者进行适应微调时，容易破坏原有的语义结构，使得类别边界变得模糊，从而导致检索与重建性能明显下降。为解决上述问题，团队提出了一个双层对齐的跨被试解码框架 Duala。

At the stimulus level, Duala uses semantic constraints and relational consistency constraints to keep the category-level semantic geometry stable across subjects. At the subject level, it introduces a distribution perturbation mechanism that uses source-subject statistics to guide new-subject representation alignment, suppressing overfitting while adapting to individual differences.具体而言，一方面，Duala 通过刺激层的语义约束与关系一致性约束，在不同受试者之间保持类别语义几何结构稳定；另一方面，通过受试者层的分布扰动机制，利用源受试者的统计信息引导新受试者表征对齐，从而在适配个体差异的同时抑制过拟合风险。

Reconstruction Results重建结果

By combining stimulus-level consistency and subject-level alignment, Duala preserves semantic structure while adapting to individual differences, leading to robust and generalizable cross-subject decoding. The reconstruction examples show that Duala can generate images that better reflect the semantic categories of the original stimuli, reducing misclassification and cross-category confusion compared with previous methods.通过结合刺激层一致性和被试层对齐，Duala 能够在适应个体差异的同时保留语义结构，从而实现鲁棒且泛化的跨被试解码。重建结果显示，与以往方法相比，该方法能够生成更准确反映刺激物语义类别的图像，有效减少误分类和跨类别混淆。
